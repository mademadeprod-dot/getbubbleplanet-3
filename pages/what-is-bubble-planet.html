What is Bubble Planet?

Everything you need to know about the immersive experience taking the world by storm — what it is, what to expect, who it's for, and where to find it.

What is Bubble Planet?

Bubble Planet is a large-scale immersive experience designed to transport visitors into a colourful, surreal world of giant bubbles, luminous installations and interactive environments. Produced by Exhibition Hub in partnership with Fever, it has rapidly become one of the most talked-about experiences globally — with locations now spanning the UK, US, Australia, Canada and Europe.

Unlike a traditional exhibition or theme park, Bubble Planet is a walk-through experience where each room tells a different visual story. The environments are designed to appeal to all five senses, with sound, light, touch and movement all playing a role. There are no rides, no queues between rooms, and no fixed route — you explore at your own pace.

How long does Bubble Planet last?

Most visitors spend between 60 and 90 minutes inside Bubble Planet. There's no set duration — you move through the environments at your own pace. Families with young children often spend longer (90+ minutes) while solo visitors or couples may move quicker. You book a time slot for entry, not a fixed exit time.

Who is Bubble Planet for?

Bubble Planet is designed for all ages. In practice, it's most popular with:

  • Families with children (ages 3–14 especially) — kids are consistently wowed by every room
  • Couples and friend groups looking for something different to do
  • Photography and social media enthusiasts — every room is purpose-built for photos
  • Anyone who loves immersive art and light installations

Children under 2 typically enter for free. The experience is mostly wheelchair accessible, though some areas may have limitations depending on the venue.

Where is Bubble Planet?

Bubble Planet has grown rapidly since its launch and now operates in 20+ cities worldwide. Current locations include London, Manchester, Wembley, New York, Bellevue, Oakland, Washington DC, Atlanta, Seattle, Phoenix, Dallas, Denver, Sydney, Melbourne, Toronto, Montréal, Berlin, Utrecht and more.

New cities are added regularly — check the full city list for the most up-to-date availability.

How to book Bubble Planet tickets

Tickets are booked online through the official Bubble Planet booking system (powered by Fever). You select your city, choose a date and time slot, select how many tickets you need, and pay securely online. Mobile tickets are delivered by email — no printing required.

Advance booking is strongly recommended. Bubble Planet regularly sells out on weekends and during school holidays. Weekday slots tend to have better availability and are often cheaper.
